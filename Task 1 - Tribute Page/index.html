The Timeline of AB de Villers

  • 17 February 1984 :- Abraham Benjamin de Villiers born in Pretoria, Gauteng.

  • 17 December 2004 : Makes debut for the Titans in domestic cricket.

  • 16 November 2004 : Debuts in Test cricket against England.

  • 2 February 2005 : Debuts in One Day International (ODI) cricket against England.

  • 2006 : Debuts in Twenty20 International (T20I) cricket against Australia.

  • 10 April 2007 : Scores maiden ODI century (146 runs) against West Indies in the World Cup.

  • 4 April 2008 : Becomes the first South African to score a double century against India (217 runs). And goes on to score many more runs and keeps breaking many stats. He also clinches the No.1 Batsman spot.

  • 18 January 2015 : Sets records for the fastest ODI fifty (16 balls), century (31 balls), and 150 (64 balls) against West Indies.In the formats like ODI,T20 and Tests The no.1 Spot was retained by him again and again.

  • December 2016 : Steps down as Test captain due to an elbow injury.

  • 23 May 2018 : Announces retirement from all forms of international cricket. Probably the whole world was in shock and specially the proteas Team which was left with big shoes to fill up.

  • 19 November 2021 : Announces retirement from all forms of cricket, including franchise cricket. Having played the last few games of his carrer he decided to quit it very quitely without any farewell game or any guard of honour. The man who stayed at the top for so long finally stepped down.
